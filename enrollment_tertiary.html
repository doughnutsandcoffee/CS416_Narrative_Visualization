About the Data

School enrollment, gender parity index (GPI) - refers to a measure that assesses the gender balance in primary education enrollment.
  • GPI for enrollment is an important indicator of gender equality in education.
  • GPI helps identify disparities and gaps in access to education between genders.
  • Monitoring GPI allows policymakers and educators to track progress towards achieving gender equality in education enrollment.
How is GPI calculated?
  • GPI is calculated by comparing the number of girls to boys enrolled at the given education level in both public and private schools.
  • Gender parity index (GPI) is a ratio that indicates parity between girls and boys in terms of enrollment.
    • GPI = 1 indicates perfect gender parity, meaning an equal number of girls and boys are enrolled.
    • GPI > 1 indicates a higher enrollment of girls compared to boys.
    • GPI < 1 indicates a higher enrollment of boys compared to girls.

Sources: World Bank DataBank - School enrollment, primary and secondary (gross), gender parity index (GPI)
